# German Tech Interview Culture

## Interview Process Overview

A typical senior Java developer interview process at a German company:

| Round | Format | Duration | Focus |
|-------|--------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Recruiter phone screen | 30 min | Background, salary, visa |
| 2 | Technical phone/video | 45–60 min | Live coding or take-home |
| 3 | Technical on-site/video | 90–120 min | System design + deep-dive |
| 4 | Team fit / cultural | 45–60 min | Behavioral + team meeting |
| 5 | Hiring decision | — | — |

Large German companies (SAP, Zalando, N26, Delivery Hero, HelloFresh, Celonis) may have 4–5 rounds. Startups often do 2–3.

## Cultural Expectations

### Direct Communication
Germans appreciate **directness**. It is perfectly professional to say:
- *"I'm not sure about this. Let me think for a moment."*
- *"I don't know the answer off the top of my head, but here's how I'd approach finding it."*
- *"I disagree with this approach because..."*

This is not rude — it signals intellectual honesty.

### Thoroughness Over Speed
German engineers often value **doing things right** over doing them fast. In live coding:
- Take time to clarify requirements
- Structure your code well before optimizing
- Mention edge cases even if you don't implement all of them

### Formal vs Informal
- **Formal ("Sie")** vs **informal ("du")**: most tech companies use "du" internally, but use "Sie" in the first email to HR
- Start formal, switch to informal when invited to ("Wir duzen uns hier")
- First email to recruiter: formal is safe — "Guten Tag Frau/Herr [Name]"

## What German Companies Prioritize

### Clean Code & Craftsmanship
Influenced by the German engineering tradition of quality (*Qualität*). Expect follow-up questions like:
- *"How would you make this code more testable?"*
- *"What would you rename these variables for clarity?"*
- *"How would you handle this in a production environment?"*

### Long-term Thinking
Germans think in terms of maintainability and consequences. Be prepared to discuss:
- *"How would this solution scale if we 100x the data?"*
- *"What's your strategy for schema migrations?"*
- *"How would you handle backward compatibility if you change this API?"*

### Work-Life Balance
Germany has strong labor laws. It's appropriate to ask about:
- Working hours expectations
- Remote work policy
- Overtime practices

This is not seen as lacking dedication — it's seen as being realistic.

## Salary Negotiation in Germany

### Typical Ranges (2024, gross annual, Berlin/Munich)
| Level | Range |
|-------|-------|
| Mid-level (3–6 yrs) | €65,000 – €90,000 |
| Senior (6+ yrs) | €85,000 – €130,000+ |
| Staff/Principal | €120,000 – €180,000+ |

### Tips
- It's normal and expected to negotiate
- Research via Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary for the specific company
- Mention your current/competing offers if you have them
- Ask about the full package: base, bonus, equity, Urlaubstage (vacation days), Weiterbildung (training budget)
- Statutory minimum vacation is 20 days for a 5-day week (24 for a 6-day week); many tech companies *typically* offer around 28–30 days

## Work Authorization

### EU/EEA Citizens
No work permit needed. Can start immediately.

### Non-EU/EEA Citizens
- **EU Blue Card**: requires Bachelor's degree + job offer ≥ €45,552/year (2024) or €35,100 for shortage occupations (IT qualifies)
- Processing time: 6–12 weeks typically
- Most German tech companies sponsor visas — mention your situation early with the recruiter

## German Tech Company Profiles

### German Companies Known for Java
- **SAP** (Walldorf): enterprise Java, very process-oriented, formal culture
- **Bosch, BMW, Deutsche Bank, Allianz** (various): large enterprise, stable, structured
- **Zalando** (Berlin): modern tech, data-driven, English-first culture
- **N26** (Berlin): fintech, fast-paced, startup culture
- **Celonis** (Munich): process mining, strong engineering culture
- **TeamViewer** (Stuttgart): remote access software, stable

### International Companies with German HQs
- SAP, Siemens, Infineon, BASF — traditional companies transforming digitally
- Delivery Hero, Auto1, Personio — unicorns with international culture

## Interview Red Flags to Watch For
- No clear engineering culture or technical leadership
- Vague answers about tech stack or team structure
- Pressure to accept offer immediately
- No time for your questions

## Green Flags
- Engineers interviewing you (not just HR)
- Structured onboarding mentioned
- Open discussion of technical debt or past mistakes
- Clear answers about team autonomy and decision-making
